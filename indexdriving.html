Safe Driving In South Africa

Rent a Car Today

Rent vs Car-for-Hire

The key decision when determining whether to rent a car is to determine how far you will be traveling around South Africa. If you plan to stay in Cape Town for your entire trip, you are likely best off avoiding renting a car. However, if you plan to drive for hours outside of Cape Town you should consider renting a car.

= Amount of Caution Required

Driving on the Left Side

Unlike the US, you drive on the left side of the road and the drivers steering wheel is on the right. This can be disorienting.

Narrow Roads

The roads in South Africa are extremely narrow and can be dangerous as soon as you get outside CapeTown into the mountain passes. Drive slowly if you don't feel comfortable.

Parking

There is a good amount of free parking in Cape Town, but most spots have a maximum of 1 or 2 hours. Beyond that, you will likely need a private parking space. Garage parking is also inexpensive - usually $1/hr.

Uber

You can use uber in South Africa. While overall it is safe, there is less oversight than in the US and you might be better off in a taxi.

Taxi

Taxi's are the best way to go short distances. If you get in a taxi at a crowded tourist spot, you may be overcharged. For the cheapest, most reliable and safe taxi, call Excite Taxis.
