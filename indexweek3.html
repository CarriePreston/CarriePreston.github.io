Summary

In December, George Lace and I traveled to Cape Town and the surrounding area for the adventure of a lifetime. For both of us, it was our first time to Africa. This page provides trip highlights and is useful whether you're spending a quarter abroad there or just looking for your next big adventure! Enjoy :)

Our Route

Cape Town --> Stellenbosch --> Inverdoorn --> Hermanus --> Cape Town

Cape Town

With both a beach and surrounding mountains, Cape Town offers a variety of activities! My top 5 activities are:

  • Table Mountain Hike and Repelling
  • Robben Island Nelson Mandella Tour
  • Boulder Beach Penquin Colony
  • Cape Point / Cape of Good Hope
  • Kirstenbosch Botanical Gardens

Stellenbosch

Stellenbosch is known for having some of the most beautiful wineries. The great wine is followed by great cuisine, here are my top picks:

  • Ernie Els Winery
  • La Petite Ferme Winery
  • Hussan Grill
  • Verde Lust Cafe

Inverdoorn Game Reserve

The Inverdoorn Game Reserve is only 2.5 hours north of wine country and offers a unique opportunity to see four of the "Big 5" animals! Take a night-time and early morning safari drive with a tour guide to see some of Africa's most amazing wildlife. Below is a table of the animals you can expect to see at Inverdoorn:

Animals

Lions There are three lions on the reserve, one male and two female. All three have been rescued from cage hunters. Since they have never been in the wild, they do not know how to hunt and are fed each day by the caretakers of Inverdoorn
Cheetahs There are two male cheetahs in the reserve. Additionally, Inverdoorn has a cheetah rehibilitation center where they care for cheetahs before releasing them into the wild.
Rhinos There are three rhinos in the reserve which have to be carefully watched for fear of poachers. In recent years, the Rhino popoulation has been declining due the rising value of the horns, which rhinos are killed for.
Giraffes There are 16 giraffe on the reserve. 15 of them can often be found together in a pack, while one older male tends to wander around by himself since he was kicked out of the pack by a younger male giraffe.
Hippos The two hippos located on the reserve can usually be found near one of the two lakes on the reserve since they spend a majority of their time near water.
Zebras The reserve has many zebras. They are often found in packs and usually surround their young to protect them from other animals.
Springbok Springbok can be found in abundence in the reserve. They are prey of the cheetah.

Hermanus

Located along South Africa's Garden Route, Hermanus is one of South Africa's premiere beach towns. If you're adventurous, it has fantastic whale watching and shark diving opportunities.

  • Grotto Beach
  • Gansbaii Shark Diving
